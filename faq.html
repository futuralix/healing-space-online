Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our therapy services

Getting Started

How do I schedule my first appointment?

You can schedule your first appointment by calling us at (555) 123-4567, emailing contact@therapy.com, or filling out the contact form on our website. We'll get back to you within 24 hours to confirm your appointment time.

What should I expect in my first session?

Your first session is an opportunity for us to get to know you and understand your concerns. We'll discuss what brings you to therapy, your history, and your goals. This helps us create a personalized treatment plan that works for you.

How do I know if therapy is right for me?

If you're feeling overwhelmed, struggling with relationships, experiencing persistent sadness or anxiety, or simply want to improve your mental well-being, therapy can help. Schedule a consultation to discuss your specific needs and determine if therapy is a good fit.

Sessions & Appointments

How long are therapy sessions?

Standard therapy sessions are 50 minutes long. Initial consultations may be scheduled for 60-90 minutes to allow for a comprehensive assessment and to ensure we have enough time to understand your needs.

How often should I attend therapy?

Most clients benefit from weekly sessions, especially in the beginning. As progress is made, we may transition to bi-weekly sessions. The frequency depends on your individual needs and goals, which we'll discuss together.

Do you offer virtual sessions?

Yes, we offer secure telehealth sessions via video conferencing for your convenience. Virtual sessions are just as effective as in-person therapy for most concerns and provide greater flexibility in scheduling.

What is your cancellation policy?

We require 24 hours notice for cancellations or rescheduling. Late cancellations or no-shows may be charged the full session fee. We understand that emergencies happen and will work with you on a case-by-case basis.

Insurance & Fees

Do you accept insurance?

Yes, we accept most major insurance plans including Blue Cross Blue Shield, Aetna, Cigna, UnitedHealthcare, and others. Please contact us to verify if we accept your specific plan. We can also provide superbills for out-of-network reimbursement.

What are your fees?

Session fees vary depending on the type of service. We also offer a sliding scale for clients experiencing financial hardship. Please contact us directly to discuss fees and payment options that work for your situation.

Do you offer sliding scale fees?

Yes, we offer sliding scale fees based on financial need. We believe everyone should have access to quality mental health care, and we're committed to working with you to find an affordable option.

Privacy & Confidentiality

Is everything I share confidential?

Yes, therapy is completely confidential. What you share in sessions is protected by law. The only exceptions are situations involving immediate danger to yourself or others, suspected abuse of a child or vulnerable adult, or when required by court order.

Are telehealth sessions secure?

Absolutely. We use HIPAA-compliant, encrypted video conferencing platforms to ensure your privacy and security during virtual sessions. Your personal information and session content are fully protected.

Treatment Approach

What therapeutic approaches do you use?

We use evidence-based approaches including Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), mindfulness-based techniques, psychodynamic therapy, and solution-focused therapy. We tailor our approach to your unique needs and preferences.

How long does therapy take?

The duration of therapy varies greatly depending on your goals and concerns. Some people find relief in a few months, while others benefit from longer-term work. We'll regularly review your progress and adjust the treatment plan as needed.

Can I bring my partner or family member?

Yes, we offer couples counseling and family therapy sessions. We can also invite family members to individual sessions when appropriate and with your consent. Including loved ones can be beneficial for many therapeutic goals.

Special Situations

What if I need help between sessions?

For urgent situations, please call our office. If you're experiencing a mental health emergency, please call 911 or go to your nearest emergency room. We also provide a list of crisis resources during your first session.

Do you prescribe medication?

As therapists, we do not prescribe medication. However, we work closely with psychiatrists and can provide referrals if medication might be helpful as part of your treatment plan.

What if I don't feel like we're a good fit?

The therapeutic relationship is crucial to success. If you feel we're not the right fit, please let us know. We can discuss your concerns and, if needed, provide referrals to other therapists who might be better suited to your needs.

Still Have Questions?

We're here to help. Reach out and we'll answer any questions you might have.
